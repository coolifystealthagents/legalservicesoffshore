# Homepage design research

## Brief

- Search query: `legal process outsourcing services for law firms`
- Research date: 2026-07-20
- Audience: law firms and legal teams that want offshore support for intake, matter admin, document preparation, billing support, and quality reporting
- Conversion goal: send qualified visitors to the existing staffing-plan contact flow
- Public pricing: none

## Five relevant competitors

### 1. Legal Support World

URL: https://www.legalsupportworld.com/

Useful patterns:

- Strong legal category recognition through navy, red, and editorial law imagery
- Specific service grid for litigation, contracts, paralegal work, research, and document review
- Early proof band with practice-area, client, coverage, and team metrics
- Software familiarity and security claims help answer buyer concerns

Weak spots:

- The hero is vague and has no direct CTA
- Dense copy and repeated section patterns make the page long
- Generic justice imagery and unclear card controls reduce the premium feel

### 2. Cogneesol legal solutions

URL: https://www.cogneesol.com/legal-services

Useful patterns:

- Controlled corporate palette and clean two-column hero
- Strong compliance presence in the footer
- International contact details make the operation feel established
- Simple top-right consultation CTA

Weak spots:

- The page does not show concrete legal services early enough
- Large empty areas made the live desktop page feel unfinished
- The stock legal-tech art is relevant but not very human

### 3. SunLexis

URL: https://www.sunlexis.com/

Useful patterns:

- Clear split between service types and legal practice areas
- Quote form is short and easy to understand
- Case studies, articles, and a legal-services disclaimer support trust
- Navy and lime create a recognizable system

Weak spots:

- The page is long, dated, and heavy on stock legal imagery
- The hero headline is generic and lacks an integrated primary CTA
- Proof is asserted more often than shown

### 4. Get Staffed Up

URL: https://getstaffedup.com/

Useful patterns:

- Direct, short headline built around the buyer's time problem
- Real staff imagery makes remote support feel tangible
- Law-firm-specific pain points lead naturally into the offer
- Clear CTA repetition, role presentation, and managed-support framing
- Strong hierarchy with large type, generous spacing, and warm human photography

Weak spots:

- Some sections use aggressive savings and retention claims that require strong proof
- The navigation and full page contain more choices than this smaller site needs
- Its exact carousel, trade dress, and branded language should not be copied

### 5. Hire Paralegals

URL: https://hireparalegals.com/

Useful patterns:

- Immediate legal-talent positioning
- Concrete talent cards show skills, tools, experience, and availability
- Two clear hero actions and a visible reassurance panel
- Search and specialty paths help visitors self-select

Weak spots:

- Public pricing and marketplace mechanics do not fit this site's managed inquiry model
- Large savings claims and profile ratings need proof
- The marketplace card density can feel transactional for firms seeking a controlled support plan

## Selected inspiration

Get Staffed Up has the strongest overall homepage design for this project. Its short problem-led hero, human staff imagery, law-firm-specific copy, and managed-support story make the service easy to understand. It also builds a cleaner path from pain to role fit to consultation than the broader LPO sites.

## Original implementation direction

The new homepage borrows principles, not trade dress:

- Use a restrained ink, parchment, copper, and sage palette instead of Get Staffed Up's brand colors
- Use a case-file editorial type system with a serif display face and plain sans-serif body copy
- Replace staff carousels and public profiles with one legal-work image plus role-scope notes
- Lead with legal admin ownership and attorney review boundaries, not freedom or savings claims
- Present four existing service routes as case-file cards rather than marketplace talent cards
- Use the site's existing contact flow and keep all pricing private
- Keep the existing simple logo, routes, footer, privacy, terms, cancellation policy, and copyright

No competitor copy, logos, profiles, proprietary illustrations, or distinctive branded layouts are reproduced.
